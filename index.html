Mark Ribisi

President & CEO

LA Fires

On Tuesday, January 7th, the unimaginable happened. A strong Santa Ana wind event coupled with a historically dry winter, created a tinderbox in Southern California. Late in the morning, a small fire in the Santa Monica mountains quickly became a firestorm sweeping through Pacific Palisades and parts of Malibu.

Videos of homes burning on Pacific Coast Highway and people desperate to escape were gut-wrenching. Later that evening as the wind approached 80-90 miles per hour, a fire started in Eaton Canyon east of Altadena and north of Pasadena and once again, quickly became a firestorm, overwhelming the stretched firefighting resources in the area. Additional fires in Sylmar and the Hollywood Hills added to the overall angst of the event.

The fires have been a devastating force, causing widespread destruction, displacement, and disillusionment among civic leaders. As an insurance company and agency, we play a crucial role in aiding recovery and rebuilding efforts by addressing the immediate needs of our affected policyholders and implementing longer-term strategies to mitigate future risks.

From Mercury's perspective, Gabe Tirador, our CEO, said it best, "For us, this is 'Game Time.' It is a great opportunity to demonstrate our purpose: 'We help our customers prepare for, and overcome life's unexpected challenges."

Insurance companies, including Mercury, mobilized disaster response teams to assist policyholders in filing claims and expediting the process. Claims adjusters were deployed to assess damage, estimate repair costs, and determine appropriate compensation, ensuring swift claims processing, reducing the financial burden on those affected. Additional Living Expenses (ALE) payments were disbursed, covering temporary housing and other necessities while policyholders rebuild their homes.

Going forward, risk mitigation will play a larger role in access to California homeowners insurance products. Most insurance companies promote fire-resistant building practices through incentives like premium discounts, reducing the overall risk of fire damage. They also invest in community education and preparedness programs, raising awareness about fire safety and protection.

Wildfires have become a regular, year-round event in California, and all parties need to improve their response to them. Collaboration between insurers, policymakers, and other stakeholders is vital. Efforts to improve building codes, enhance fire prevention measures, and invest in large-scale mitigation projects are essential. Public-private partnerships can help share the financial burden and ensure adequate resources for disaster response and recovery.

It's a great time to be AIS and Mercury!
