A Message From the President:

Mark Ribisi

President & CEO

2026 – The Year of Milestones

Celebrating Our Historic Journey

As we step into 2026, we are poised to celebrate a year filled with remarkable milestones and cherished memories. This marks our 58th year in business, tracing our roots back to 1968 when our founder, Jim Caird, wrote our very first insurance policy. From that humble beginning, our company has grown and evolved through changes in ownership and strategic decisions, ultimately achieving a direct written premium of over $950 million in 2025. With continued dedication and effort, we anticipate surpassing the $1 billion threshold in direct written premium this year.

Manhattan Beach 1960s

Putting Our Achievement in Perspective

To genuinely appreciate this milestone, consider that over 1,800 companies in the United States have a valuation exceeding $1 billion. Among US Fire and Casualty companies, roughly 200 write more than $1 billion in annual direct written premium. In our own independent agency and brokerage space, only a select group—10 to 15 agencies or brokerages—have achieved this level. Notable examples include Marsh, Aon, Gallagher, Hub, Acrisure, Brightways, and Confie, all of whom reached this benchmark through mergers or acquisitions. For our agency, which began as a one- man operation on Highland Ave in Manhattan Beach CA across from the fire station, reaching this milestone is a significant accomplishment.

The Meaning Behind Milestones

While milestones mark important moments in our journey, they are not the pinnacle of our story. The real value lies in the experiences and lessons learned between these achievements—the hard work, perseverance, and sometimes a bit of luck that got us here. Our company’s fortunes changed in 2009 when Mercury General purchased AIS, recognizing our role as a key distribution partner. Under the stewardship of leaders such as Mr. George Joseph, Gabe Tirador, Ted Stalick, and now Victor Joseph, Mercury has helped us realize our potential and expand our reach. Today, nearly $225 million in direct written premium is placed with other carriers, including many states where Mercury does not compete.

Looking Ahead: Embracing Change and Growth

To reach our next milestone, we must continue to embrace change and welcome innovations in artificial intelligence and technology. Listening to our customers, supporting the professional development of our team members, and staying true to our mission of helping people with their insurance needs will be essential for our continued success.

Honoring Our Country’s 250th Anniversary

On July 4, 2026, our nation will celebrate its 250th anniversary—our semi- quincentennial. In a time of instant gratification and occasional dissatisfaction, it is vital to remember the freedoms we enjoy. These freedoms enabled a second-generation immigrant to defend our country in WW2 and eventually to found Mercury, and an once down-on-his-luck insurance salesman to establish AIS. The possibilities remain endless.

Looking Forward to 2026

With all these milestones ahead, I am excited for a successful and memorable 2026.

Let's Talk Sales:

Mark Casas

Senior Vice President

Starting 2025

I wanted to take a moment to share some exciting news about our performance in December 2025. We closed the year with strong results across most key metrics compared to both prior months and December 2024.

Our total sales reached an impressive 12,158, up from 10,673 in December 2024, making it the second-highest monthly total in 2025. This result represents 114% of the prior year and 132% of our plan. Auto sales were particularly strong, with 8,626 in December, an increase from 7,949 last year and 131% of plan. Our homeowner sales also saw a significant jump to 3,056, up from 2,252 in December 2024, achieving 136% of the prior year and 117% of plan.

Quoting activity also finished strong. Auto quotes for December were 19,366, up from 16,850 in 2024 and 109% of plan. Homeowners quotes reached 10,331, a substantial increase over 6,303 last year and 121% of plan. Our closing ratios remained robust, with the auto closing ratio at 44.54%, slightly below December 2024 but well above plan. The homeowners closing ratio improved to 29.58%, up from the 24.47% average for the year, and significantly higher than plan.

These results cap a year of growth, with notable gains in both sales and quoting activity, and strong closing performance across all lines. Thank you all for an outstanding year of hard work and dedication. Your efforts have been instrumental in leading the way toward our goal of hitting $1 billion in written premium by 2026.

Let's continue this momentum into the new year and achieve even greater success together!

Rusella Hernandez

30 Years of Service Excellence

This month, we’re thrilled to honor Rusella Hernandez as she celebrates an amazing 30 years of service excellence with AIS! Rusella kicked off her AIS journey on January 4, 1996, starting as a File Clerk. It didn’t take long for her natural customer service superpowers to shine, and soon she stepped into handling policy renewals. From there, she moved into underwriting, wore multiple hats across the service team, and today continues to delight customers as a Sr. Customer Relations Specialist. Even with all the roles she’s taken on, she admits she sometimes misses renewals…that job always guaranteed happy customers because she could offer better rates and great options.

Cesar Contreras commended: Congratulations on reaching 30 years with AIS, Rusella! What an awesome milestone. This achievement speaks volumes about your dedication and commitment. Our customers consistently express their appreciation for your approachable nature and clarity you bring to the insurance process. Your amazing work ethic, professionalism and first-call resolution approach have set a standard that inspires everyone around you. It’s been a pleasure to work alongside you and get to know you over the past few years. Congrats again!

When she’s not working, Rusella enjoys bowling, getting involved in community activities, and staying active with her church. A few fun facts: her favorite movie is Pretty Woman, she loves Italian food, and she’s a huge Lakers fan! She hopes to travel more soon, with Switzerland at the top of her list. And if she weren’t at AIS, she says she’d likely be crunching numbers in an accounting or bookkeeping career.

As Rusella reflects on her impressive 30 year journey, one thing stands out above all else: the people. As she puts it, “The people from day one. My coworkers, every single one of them. We’re a family…we really are. Even though we work remotely and I don’t see them often, our team meetings are always fun. It really is all about the people.” We couldn’t agree more!

Please join us in celebrating this amazing milestone with Rusella! Her passion, dedication, and allaround awesomeness continue to inspire everyone around her.

Congratulations, Rusella — here’s to 30 incredible years and many more to come!

Happy New Year!

Here’s to a fantastic 2026 filled with laughter, creativity, and adventure! With winter in full swing, it’s the perfect time to embrace the season and stay physically active, mentally engaged, and cozy-happy—whether you’re inside or out.

Cozy Indoor Fun

When it’s chilly outside, turn your home into the ultimate winter hangout:

• Movie or Series Marathon: Pick a theme, grab the popcorn, and settle in.

• Game Night Showdown: Board games, cards, charades, or video games—winner gets bragging rights.

• Crafty Creations: Make paper snowflakes, pinecone bird feeders, or build a blanket fort worthy of legends.

• Cook & Bake Together: Try an international recipe or whip up family favorites.

• Storytime Magic: Share family stories by a fire (or candle!) or curl up with a great book.

• Home Spa Day: Winter-themed bath bombs, face masks, and total relaxation.

• Learn Something New: Take an online class, learn an instrument, or explore a virtual destination.

Outdoor Winter Adventures

Bundle up and enjoy the crisp air—snow or no snow!

• Ice Skating & Hockey: Public skates or friendly games at local rinks.

• Snow Day Fun (if you have snow): Sledding, snowshoeing, tubing, snowball fights, or building epic snowmen.

• Winter Walks & Hikes: Quieter trails, birdwatching, frozen waterfalls, and hot chocolate rewards.

• Snow-Free Exploring: Hiking, biking, or geocaching on peaceful winter trails.

• Stargazing: Clear winter nights = brighter stars and magical views.

• Nature Watching: Feed birds, spot wildlife, or enjoy the stillness.

• Bonfires & Warm Drinks: S'mores, hot cocoa, and cozy conversations.

• Unique Winter Fun: Ice lanterns, snow bowling, ice fishing, or even dog sledding if available!

No matter how you spend it, winter is full of ways to play, relax, and reconnect. So grab a blanket, lace up your boots, and make this season unforgettable!
